# Stakeholder Analysis

## Overview

This document identifies all stakeholders involved in the Abqar Store Sales Management System project, analyzes their roles, needs, and expectations, and provides a stakeholder influence and interest matrix to guide project communication and engagement strategies.

## Stakeholder Identification

### Primary Stakeholders

#### 1. Store Owner/Manager
- **Role**: Primary decision maker and system owner
- **Responsibilities**: 
  - Final approval of system features and changes
  - Budget allocation and resource management
  - Strategic business decisions
- **Needs**: 
  - Comprehensive sales analytics and reporting
  - Real-time business performance monitoring
  - Cost-effective solution with clear ROI
  - Easy-to-use management interface
- **Expectations**:
  - Improved operational efficiency
  - Reduced manual paperwork
  - Better inventory management
  - Increased sales tracking accuracy

#### 2. Sales Staff/Cashiers
- **Role**: Primary system users for daily operations
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Process customer transactions
  - Manage customer interactions
  - Update inventory information
  - Generate sales reports
- **Needs**:
  - Intuitive and fast transaction processing
  - Quick customer lookup and management
  - Easy inventory checking
  - Minimal training requirements
- **Expectations**:
  - Faster checkout process
  - Reduced errors in transactions
  - Better customer service capabilities
  - User-friendly interface

#### 3. Customers
- **Role**: End users of the retail service
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Make purchases
  - Provide feedback on service quality
  - Participate in loyalty programs
- **Needs**:
  - Fast and accurate checkout process
  - Transparent pricing and billing
  - Loyalty program benefits
  - Good customer service experience
- **Expectations**:
  - Reduced waiting time
  - Accurate billing and receipts
  - Personalized service through customer history
  - Reliable system availability

### Secondary Stakeholders

#### 4. IT Support/System Administrator
- **Role**: Technical maintenance and support
- **Responsibilities**:
  - System installation and configuration
  - Regular maintenance and updates
  - Troubleshooting technical issues
  - Data backup and security management
- **Needs**:
  - Clear system documentation
  - Remote access capabilities
  - Automated backup systems
  - Error logging and monitoring tools
- **Expectations**:
  - Stable and reliable system performance
  - Easy maintenance procedures
  - Comprehensive error reporting
  - Scalable architecture

#### 5. Accountant/Financial Manager
- **Role**: Financial oversight and reporting
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Financial report generation
  - Tax compliance and reporting
  - Budget analysis and forecasting
  - Audit trail maintenance
- **Needs**:
  - Accurate financial reporting
  - Integration with accounting systems
  - Audit trail capabilities
  - Tax-compliant documentation
- **Expectations**:
  - Automated financial calculations
  - Comprehensive reporting features
  - Data integrity and accuracy
  - Compliance with financial regulations

#### 6. Inventory Manager
- **Role**: Stock management and procurement
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Monitor inventory levels
  - Manage stock replenishment
  - Track product performance
  - Coordinate with suppliers
- **Needs**:
  - Real-time inventory tracking
  - Low stock alerts and notifications
  - Product performance analytics
  - Supplier management integration
- **Expectations**:
  - Accurate inventory counts
  - Automated reorder notifications
  - Better demand forecasting
  - Reduced stockouts and overstock

### External Stakeholders

#### 7. Suppliers/Vendors
- **Role**: Product and service providers
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Provide products and services
  - Maintain supply chain reliability
  - Support integration requirements
- **Needs**:
  - Order processing integration
  - Payment processing capabilities
  - Communication channels for updates
- **Expectations**:
  - Timely order processing
  - Accurate order information
  - Reliable payment processing

#### 8. Regulatory Bodies
- **Role**: Compliance and regulation enforcement
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Ensure tax compliance
  - Monitor business operations
  - Enforce industry regulations
- **Needs**:
  - Accurate transaction records
  - Tax reporting capabilities
  - Audit trail maintenance
- **Expectations**:
  - Compliance with tax regulations
  - Accurate financial reporting
  - Transparent business operations

#### 9. Technology Partners (Firebase, Third-party Services)
- **Role**: Platform and service providers
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Provide reliable cloud services
  - Ensure data security and privacy
  - Offer technical support
- **Needs**:
  - Proper API usage
  - Security best practices implementation
  - Service level agreement compliance
- **Expectations**:
  - Responsible resource usage
  - Security compliance
  - Proper error handling

## Stakeholder Needs and Expectations Analysis

### Business Impact Requirements
- **High Priority**: Store Owner, Sales Staff, Customers
- **Medium Priority**: IT Support, Accountant, Inventory Manager
- **Low Priority**: Suppliers, Regulatory Bodies, Technology Partners

### Technical Requirements Impact
- **High Priority**: IT Support, Technology Partners
- **Medium Priority**: Store Owner, Sales Staff
- **Low Priority**: Customers, Suppliers, Regulatory Bodies

### Functional Requirements Impact
- **High Priority**: Sales Staff, Store Owner, Customers
- **Medium Priority**: Accountant, Inventory Manager
- **Low Priority**: IT Support, Suppliers

## Stakeholder Influence and Interest Matrix

### High Influence, High Interest (Manage Closely)
- **Store Owner/Manager**: Primary decision maker with direct business impact
- **Sales Staff/Cashiers**: Daily users whose adoption determines system success

### High Influence, Low Interest (Keep Satisfied)
- **IT Support/System Administrator**: Technical authority but may not be directly involved in business decisions
- **Accountant/Financial Manager**: Financial oversight authority but focused on specific reporting needs

### Low Influence, High Interest (Keep Informed)
- **Customers**: Directly affected by system performance but no decision-making power
- **Inventory Manager**: Highly interested in inventory features but limited overall influence

### Low Influence, Low Interest (Monitor)
- **Suppliers/Vendors**: Minimal direct interaction with the system
- **Regulatory Bodies**: Compliance requirements but indirect involvement
- **Technology Partners**: Service providers with standard service agreements

## Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

### Communication Plan
1. **Weekly Updates**: Store Owner, Sales Staff
2. **Bi-weekly Reports**: IT Support, Accountant, Inventory Manager
3. **Monthly Summaries**: Customers (through store communications)
4. **As-needed Basis**: Suppliers, Regulatory Bodies, Technology Partners

### Feedback Mechanisms
- **Direct Meetings**: Store Owner, IT Support
- **User Training Sessions**: Sales Staff
- **Surveys and Feedback Forms**: Customers
- **Email Updates**: All other stakeholders

### Risk Mitigation
- **Resistance to Change**: Comprehensive training and gradual rollout
- **Technical Issues**: Strong IT support and backup procedures
- **Business Disruption**: Phased implementation and parallel systems
- **Compliance Issues**: Regular audits and compliance checks
